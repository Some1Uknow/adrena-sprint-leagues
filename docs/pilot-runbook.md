# Pilot Runbook

## Goal

Run a small private Sprint League and validate one core question:

`Does this format feel more fair, more readable, and more replayable than a plain leaderboard race?`

## Recommended pilot setup

- duration: `72 hours`
- cohort size: `5-15 traders`
- divisions: at least `Rookie` and `Challenger`
- reward: small fixed prize plus public recognition
- observation window: full sprint plus a short post-event survey

This is intentionally lightweight. The purpose of the pilot is not statistical certainty. The purpose is to surface whether the format creates the right product behavior.

## What to validate

- did traders understand how score was earned
- did divisions make the event feel more fair
- did liquidation penalties change behavior
- did the format increase repeat leaderboard check-ins
- did quests or streaks make the sprint feel more game-like
- would traders want to join the next sprint

## Feedback prompts

- what part of the scoring felt most fair
- what part of the scoring felt least intuitive
- did you change position sizing because of the liquidation penalty
- would you join this format again next week
- did division placement feel accurate
- which reward incentive mattered most

## Metrics to track

- opt-in rate
- repeat visits to leaderboard
- number of eligible traders
- average trades per participant
- liquidation count
- share of participants who completed at least one quest
- percentage of users who say they would replay the format

## Suggested pilot agenda

1. Invite traders and explain the scoring on one page.
2. Launch a `72-hour` sprint.
3. Publish one midpoint leaderboard update.
4. Close the sprint and publish final standings.
5. Collect written feedback immediately after close.
6. Compare replay intent against current competition formats.

## What success looks like

- traders understand the scoring without a long explanation
- divisions feel reasonable rather than random
- abusive behavior is visible and unattractive
- the midpoint update creates return visits
- most participants say they would replay the format

## What not to fake

Do not claim live pilot feedback unless real users actually ran through the event. For submission purposes, this document should be presented as the pilot plan unless a real private test was completed before submission.
